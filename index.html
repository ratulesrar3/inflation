Price Inflation & Volatility in CPI

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is often referred to as the source of truth for purchasing power in the United States. CPI expresses the year over year change in price of a basket of goods, and is reported each fiscal quarter by the Bureau of Labor Statisitics (BLS).

Additionally, the CPI is an aggregate of prices indexes for specific goods and services, which each have their own volatility.

In the chart below, what looks like a trendline for quarterly CPI values is actually a daily index calculation based online prices by MIT's Billion Prices Project (BPP). Learn more about the BPP here!

In the chart above, take a look at three sectors that comprise the CPI: food, housing, and energy.

Clearly, there has been lots of volatility in energy prices, so one way to mitigate the effect of volatile markets is by calculating the index each quarter. This has some benefits, but daily index calculations make it easier to predict future price changes.
