Price Inflation

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is often referred to as the source of truth for purchasing power in the United States.

CPI expresses the year over year change in price of a basket of goods, and is reported each fiscal quarter by the Bureau of Labor Statisitics (BLS).

In the chart below, what looks like a trendline for quarterly CPI values is actually a daily index calculation based online prices by MIT's Billion Prices Project (BPP).

See for yourself by using the brush to zoom in!

We see a larger difference between daily and quarterly calculations, especially between 2014-2015.

This may be related to the emergence of Amazon capturing a greater share of online retail, which may have increased prices online.

Volatility in CPI

The CPI is an aggregate of prices indexes for specific goods and services, which have their own volatility.

Take a look at three sectors that comprise the CPI: food, housing, and energy.
